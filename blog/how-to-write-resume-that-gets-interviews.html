How to Write a Resume That Actually Gets You Interviews

August 18, 2025 5 min read By StoryCV Team

The harsh truth? Most resumes get rejected in just 6 seconds. That's how long hiring managers spend on their initial review before deciding whether to continue reading or move to the next candidate.

But here's the good news: once you understand what hiring managers are actually looking for in those crucial first seconds, you can craft a resume that stops them in their tracks and compels them to pick up the phone.

The 6-Second Resume Scan: What Hiring Managers Actually Look For

Research shows that hiring managers follow a predictable pattern when scanning resumes. They look for:

  • Job title and company names - To quickly assess relevance
  • Employment dates - To identify gaps or tenure
  • Education credentials - For role requirements
  • Skills and keywords - To match job requirements

If these elements aren't immediately clear and compelling, your resume gets discarded—regardless of how qualified you actually are.

Strategy 1: Lead with Impact, Not Duties

The biggest mistake job seekers make is listing what they did instead of what they achieved. Compare these examples:

❌ Duty-focused (weak):

"Responsible for managing social media accounts and creating content."

✅ Impact-focused (strong):

"Increased social media engagement by 150% and drove 2,000+ qualified leads through strategic content campaigns."

Notice how the second example immediately tells the hiring manager the value this person delivered. Always start bullet points with action verbs and include quantifiable results whenever possible.

Strategy 2: Master the ATS (Applicant Tracking System)

Before your resume even reaches human eyes, it must pass through ATS software. Here's how to optimize for both robots and humans:

ATS-Friendly Formatting:

  • Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)
  • Include section headers like "Work Experience" and "Education"
  • Avoid tables, graphics, and fancy formatting
  • Save as both .docx and .pdf versions

Keyword Optimization:

Study the job posting and naturally incorporate key terms throughout your resume. If the posting mentions "project management," don't just say "managed projects"—use the exact phrase "project management."

Strategy 3: The Power of the Professional Summary

Your professional summary is prime real estate—it's often the first thing hiring managers read after your name. Make it count with this formula:

Years of Experience + Key Skills + Biggest Achievement = Compelling Summary

Example:

"Results-driven marketing manager with 7+ years of experience in digital campaigns and brand strategy. Led cross-functional teams to deliver 40% increase in qualified leads and $2M+ in new revenue. Expertise in marketing automation, analytics, and customer acquisition."

Strategy 4: Quantify Everything

Numbers grab attention and provide context. Instead of vague statements, include specific metrics:

  • Revenue generated or saved
  • Percentage improvements
  • Team sizes managed
  • Projects completed
  • Deadlines met or beaten

If you don't have exact numbers, make reasonable estimates based on your knowledge of the business impact.

Strategy 5: Tailor for Every Application

The days of "one resume fits all" are over. Top candidates customize their resumes for each application by:

  • Reordering bullet points to emphasize relevant experience
  • Adjusting keywords to match the job posting
  • Highlighting specific skills mentioned in the requirements
  • Modifying the professional summary for the role

This doesn't mean rewriting everything—small, strategic adjustments can make a huge difference.

Start implementing these strategies today:

  1. Audit your current resume - Does it lead with impact or duties?
